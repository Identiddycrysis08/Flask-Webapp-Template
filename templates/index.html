The Local Area

The Dordogne, land of 1001 chateaux and a region bursting with charm it's no wonder it draws tourists from all over the world each year. You really will be spoilt for choice if you can tear yourselves away from the lovely pool.


There are a number of lake beaches in the local area which will prove to be a big hit with all family members. With on-site restaurants, water activities, playing facilities, lakeside walks and of course the lake beach itself. The Lake at Saint Estephe even has a little foot ferry to cross the lake which is great for all family members to get involved with. They offer complimentary canoeing and paddle boarding during peak season with a life guard on duty. The food huts behind the lake will keep everyone happy serving hot and cold food, ice-creams, crepes to name but a few tasty treats. There is even a night food market by the lake during July and August.


Brantome en Perigord, the jewel of Perigord vert and known locally as the little Venice of Perigord. This charming town is home to one of the oldest bell towers in France and the stunning abbey which dominates the town. Take a tour of the abbey, caves, climb the bell tower, take a boat trip, canoe, paddle board or just stroll through the pretty streets and boutique shops. If you are celebrating a special occasion, or are a foodie you may like to dine at the Michelin star restaurant Le Moulin de l'Abbaye https://www.moulinabbaye.com During peak season the town hosts river jousting on Friday nights in front of the abbey on the River Dronne. A really fun activity to watch that draws a big crowd and creates a wonderful atmosphere on a warm summers evening.


Bourdeilles, just a very short distance from Brantome en Perigord is another charming village to wander around, take a pic nic by the river while the canoes gentle paddle by. Home to two chateaux which are open to the public.


Saint Jean de Cole, listed as one of the prettiest villages in France and sits on the River Cole. This is a charming little village to explore, with it's beautiful chateau and an ideal place to stop for some lunch or a crepe. Close by you will find Grotte de Villars https://grotte-villars.com home to a network of impressive caves where you can take a guided tour, enjoy the family animations and prehistoric gardens.


The capital of Perigord Vert is a town called Nontron, sitting high above the River Bandiat. Famous for knives, the Nontron knife is the oldest French knife originating from the 15th Century. It is still possible to see these knives in production today by visiting https://nontron-knife-france.com/la-coutellerie Home also to the Metiers d'Art where you can view and buy beautiful crafts made by local artisans.


French markets are an important part of French life. There are some very good markets in the area including Brantome, Bergerac, Piegut Pluviers and Riberac to name but a few. There are usually opportunities to sample some local produce as you wander through the stalls immersing yourselves in French life and the busy hub-bub of the morning markets.


Products produced locally include: goats cheese, honey, walnuts, walnut oil, walnut cake and biscuits, duck, foie gras, strawberries and of course wine! A visit just over the boarder to the Charante region will allow you to discover the wonderful world of Cognac, visit some vineyards and claos with some Cognac tasting. Don't forget to try the Pineau, a locally produced aperitif.


During July and August the region comes alive with different events and activities. Popular evening events are the night markets, or as they are correctly called Marches de Producteur. These events are popular with locals and tourists alike, You purchase produce directly from local producers and then have it cooked in front of you. Find a table and enjoy the atmosphere and great local food. Often these events will have live music.


It is impossible to list every opportunity, but this gives you a taste of what the region can offer you. Calling into the local tourist offices will ensure you can make the most of your holiday.


Other activities in the region for the family include, archery, boat trips, horse riding, adventure park, Velo rail and Go-Karting.
